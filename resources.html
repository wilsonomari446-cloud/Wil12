Key Financial Formulas

Net Present Value (NPV)

NPV = Σ(CF_t ÷ (1+r)^t) - Initial Investment

Evaluate investment decisions by calculating the present value of future cash flows. Positive NPV indicates a good investment.

Debt-to-Equity Ratio

D/E = Total Debt ÷ Total Equity

Assess a company's financial leverage. Lower ratios indicate less financial risk, while higher ratios show more debt-financing.

Return on Equity (ROE)

ROE = Net Income ÷ Shareholders' Equity

Measure how effectively a company uses shareholder capital. Higher ROE indicates better management and profitability.

Current Ratio

Current Ratio = Current Assets ÷ Current Liabilities

Evaluate short-term liquidity. A ratio above 1.0 suggests the company can cover short-term obligations.

Earnings Per Share (EPS)

EPS = Net Income ÷ Number of Shares

Track profitability per share. Growing EPS over time indicates improving company performance and value.

Financial Glossary

Asset: Any resource owned by an individual or company that has economic value. Examples include stocks, bonds, real estate, and cash.
Bear Market: A prolonged period when stock prices decline, typically by 20% or more from recent highs. Opposite of bull market.
Bull Market: A prolonged period of rising stock prices and investor optimism. Opposite of bear market.
Dividend: A portion of company profits paid to shareholders, usually quarterly or annually. Represents income from stock ownership.
Equity: Ownership stake in a company, represented by shares of stock. Also refers to assets minus liabilities on a balance sheet.
Index: A collection of securities representing a market segment, like the S&P 500. Used as a benchmark for market performance.
Liquidity: How quickly and easily an asset can be converted to cash without significant loss of value.
Portfolio: A collection of investments held by an individual or institution, typically diversified across asset classes.
Volatility: The degree of price fluctuation of a security or market. Higher volatility indicates greater risk and potential return.
Yield: The income return on an investment, typically expressed as a percentage. Common with bonds and dividend-paying stocks.
